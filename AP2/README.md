In order to pratice some class learned teorical knology and test our pratical and organization habilit, our professor propose a simple pratice described below and some tips for better performance:

> (text is originally in portuguese)
>
> **TIPS**
>
> To perform the exercise, follow the **"steps to success"**:
> Step 1: Problem Analysis;
>
> - Study and define the input variables;
> - Study and define the output variables;
>
> Step 2: Truth Table;
>
> - Construct the truth table according to the instructions;
> - Choose the appropriate outputs used, use the "optional" bit, but be careful not to hinder rather than help;
> - Outputs at "0" (zero) are not optional, they will always be zero.
> - Unused outputs that can be optional.
>
> Step 3: Simplified Expressions;
>
> - Reduce the expressions to the smallest number of gates;
> - You can simplify using Boolean and De Morgan algebra, but try using Karnaugh maps;
> - Remember that during simplification, the circuit may contain 3 or 4-input logic gates, so redesign the circuit using components available for purchase (2-input logic gates are easier to buy);
> - At this point, run the simulation in Circuit Maker, check its operation, and print all simulations;
> - Print especially the breadboard assembly simulation, remembering to include the pin numbers of each integrated circuit to facilitate assembly;
> - Print the datasheets and take them with you in case the circuit falls apart, avoid looking up this information on your phone.
>
> Step 4: Assembling the Circuit on the Protoboard;
>
> - Build a reduced-scale circuit using the components and logic gates available for purchase;
> - At this point, make a shopping list that includes everything needed for the practical exercise (integrated circuits, resistors, displays, jumper wire, LEDs, power supply, and protoboard);
> - Assemble and test the circuit at home;
>
